AI-powered Service Finder

A UX case study on building trust, transparency, and actionability into Montréal’s digital activity discovery experience.

Many travel and local activity platforms today rely on generic AI suggestions detached from cultural or contextual nuance. Our challenge: design a transparent, explainable, and community-driven system that builds user trust through ethical AI and local participation.

Understanding the challenge

Finding things to do in Montréal can be confusing for both tourists and locals. While many travel search platforms and AI-powered tools exist, they are often generic, inaccurate, or detached from local context.

Tourists frequently face issues such as:

  • Outdated or incomplete event and venue data.
  • Recommendations that don’t match their interests, time, or location.
  • Lack of trust in AI-generated results (“Where does this info come from?”).
  • Difficulty understanding how or why suggestions are made.
  • Inability to directly act — no clear links to official sites or maps.

Locals want to discover hidden gems and community-based events that global platforms overlook. However, there’s no central, trustworthy Montréal platform that blends personalization, transparency, and community input while giving users direct, actionable access.

Problem Statement:
Design an AI-powered Montréal activity app that facilitates transparent, explainable, and actionable recommendations. It should show reasoning, cite data sources, allow local contributions, and link directly to bookings or maps — building trust through ethical design.

Research and analysis

To understand how users find activities and what gaps exist in AI-based travel tools, we analyzed major platforms using AI for trip or event recommendations. We focused on transparency, adaptability to Montréal, and user trust.

Platform Focus Strengths Weaknesses
TripPlanner.ai Trip planning (flights, hotels, itineraries) Automated full trip coverage with lodging and sightseeing. Generic, lacks local flavor; no transparency on data or logic.
GuideGeek.com Conversational travel AI Chat-like personal interaction, accessible tone. Accuracy inconsistent; opaque reasoning; few Montréal tips.
Copilot2Trip.com AI itinerary generator Modern UI, fast and dynamic. Outdated data; poor onboarding; low credibility cues.
Yelp Reviews and listings Strong user trust and community validation. Business-oriented; lacks event discovery and AI curation.
TripAdvisor Activities and global recommendations Large dataset; semi-AI experience. No direct booking or maps; limited Montréal localization.

Key Insights

  1. Lack of transparency — users can’t see how AI makes choices.
  2. Data staleness — outdated listings, few local updates.
  3. Weak trust indicators — no citations or validation.
  4. Limited Montréal context — cultural and seasonal gaps.
  5. Inactionable UX — missing direct booking and maps.

Opportunity

  • AI-guided discovery: Conversational search for Montréal events.
  • Transparency: Show reasoning and confidence scores.
  • Actionable design: Direct access to booking, maps, or contact info.
  • Community-driven freshness: Verified local data submissions.
Design Goal:
Build a Montréal-first activity finder that merges AI guidance, transparency, and local authenticity — empowering users with explainable, trustworthy recommendations.

Defining user goals

Three key user types emerged during research. Each represents a core motivation and expectation from our system.

Tourist Explorer

Weekend ideas with direct booking and authentic local vibes.

Local Discoverer

After-work options near home; up-to-date events and cafés.

Family Planner

Kid-friendly activities, clear pricing, and simple directions.
