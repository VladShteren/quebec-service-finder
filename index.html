AI-powered Service Finder

A UX case study on building trust, transparency, and actionability into Montréal’s activity discovery experience.

Most “smart” travel tools feel the same—generic suggestions, unclear reasoning, and links that go nowhere. Our goal is different: design a Montréal-first experience that’s transparent, current, and immediately useful.

Understanding the challenge

Finding things to do in Montréal is harder than it should be. Plenty of platforms and AI helpers exist, but they’re often generic, outdated, or detached from the local vibe.

What goes wrong today

  • Event and venue info is often outdated or incomplete.
  • Suggestions ignore interests, timing, or location.
  • Users don’t know where information comes from or why it’s being suggested.
  • It’s hard to act — no clear links to official sites, bookings, or maps.

Locals want truly local picks and community events — the things big platforms miss. And there’s still no single, trustworthy hub for Montréal that’s personalized, transparent, and action-ready.

Problem Statement
Build an AI-powered Montréal activity app that is transparent, explainable, and actionable. It should show the “how” and “why” behind suggestions, stay fresh through local contributions, cite credible sources, and provide direct actions like booking links and maps — all grounded in ethical, explainable AI.

Research and analysis

We reviewed AI-based travel and activity tools to see how they explain recommendations, adapt to Montréal, offer actionable next steps, and build (or erode) trust.

Platform Focus Strengths Weaknesses
TripPlanner.ai End-to-end trip planning Automates flights, hotels, and sightseeing. Too broad; thin local context; limited transparency on data or logic.
GuideGeek.com Conversational travel AI Friendly, chat-based experience. Inconsistent accuracy; opaque reasoning; few Montréal-specific tips.
Copilot2Trip.com AI itinerary generation Fast, modern UI. Outdated data; confusing onboarding; weak credibility signals.
Yelp Reviews & listings Strong community input and trust. Business-centric; not itinerary-friendly; no guided AI flow.
TripAdvisor Global activities (with AI) Large database; hybrid suggestions. Limited transparency; Montréal context is weak; actions are indirect.

What’s missing across the board

  • Transparency: Users rarely see the reasoning or sources behind suggestions.
  • Freshness: Listings go stale without local/business contributions.
  • Montréal context: Seasonal, bilingual, and neighborhood details are overlooked.
  • Trust cues: Few citations, confidence indicators, or community validation.
  • Actionability: Not enough “book now,” official links, or map actions.

What users expect

  • Clear “why this?” reasoning and source visibility.
  • Up-to-date, trustworthy, bilingual information.
  • One-tap actions: book, view on map, contact.
  • A balance of local gems and popular attractions.

Opportunity

  • AI-guided discovery: A smart, chat-assisted flow for events, cafés, and attractions.
  • Explainability: Plain-language reasons, sources, and confidence levels.
  • Actionable design: Booking links, maps, and contacts on every listing.
  • Community freshness: Let locals and businesses submit and verify updates.
  • Trust-first UX: Verified badges, timestamps, and transparent signals.
Design Goal
Deliver a Montréal-first activity finder that blends conversational AI, transparent recommendations, and direct actions — powered by local authenticity and community participation.

Defining user goals

Anna — The Curious Tourist

Goals: Find authentic experiences that match her interests, get quick suggestions without fiddling with filters, understand why things are recommended, and act fast with directions and booking — in English or French.

Key Requirements: Plain-language AI explanations (“because you liked…”), bilingual UI, Google Maps integration, visible source credibility and timestamps (“updated 2 days ago”), and simple trust cues.

Marc — The Local Explorer

Goals: Discover real local events beyond the tourist trail, see what’s nearby right now, filter by distance/type/freshness, contribute updates, and understand how the system learns his tastes.

Key Requirements: Powerful distance and freshness filters, easy uploads for users/businesses, transparent personalization (“we learned you like art shows…”), trust badges, and simple sharing.

Sarah — The Family Planner

Goals: Find safe, kid-friendly options with clear accessibility, pricing, and parking info; ensure info is reliable and recent; and book directly when possible.

Key Requirements: Verified “Family-Friendly” tag, filters for cost/distance/accessibility, direct booking integrations, freshness indicators (“verified this week”), and reassuring, plain language.

Universal Goals & Requirements

Category Goals / Needs Design & AI Requirements
Transparency & Trust Understand why something is recommended. “Explain Recommendation” panel with reasoning, sources, and confidence.
Actionability Act immediately (book, navigate, share). Buttons: “Book now,” “Check availability,” “View on Map,” “Get Directions.”
Freshness & Accuracy See current, verified info. Timestamps and community/business verification on listings.
Localization Respect Montréal’s bilingual/cultural context. English/French UI; highlight local culture, seasons, and neighborhoods.
Privacy & Control Know how preferences are used. Clear privacy notes (“Your data tailors recommendations”).
Ease of Use Find relevant activities quickly. Simplified onboarding, chatbot guidance, intuitive navigation.

Design Principles

  • Explain, don’t overwhelm: Show reasoning in plain, human language.
  • Empower action: Every suggestion comes with a clear next step.
  • Stay current: Keep content fresh and visibly verified.
  • Feel local: Reflect Montréal’s culture and community input.
  • Be inclusive: Seamless bilingual support with accessible patterns.

Prototyping
